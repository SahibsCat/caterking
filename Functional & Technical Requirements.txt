# \*\*🧾 SAHIBS CATERING - FULL WEBSITE UI & FUNCTIONAL DOCUMENT

<br/>Database workflow  
<br/>\*\*1\. Primary Service Categories

# Only Delivery: The product is delivered to the customer without any on-site service staff

# \*customizable menu

# Buffet + Service: Includes food delivery along with on-site service personnel to manage the event

# \*customizable menu

# Meal Boxes: Features a fixed menu (no customization) where customers can only increase the quantity of boxes

# Customization: A flexible option where customers can select individual items to create a personalized order collection

#

# 2\. Menu and Pricing Structure

Pricing Tiers: Most services must be offered in three distinct price sectors: Standard, Premium, and Elite.

# Dietary Options: Customers must be able to select from Veg, Non-Veg, or Combined (mixed) menus

# Meal Box Varieties: Specific categories for meal boxes include Adult, Snack, and Kids options

# Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ): Delivery and meal box services will have a base MOQ, starting at 10 people or 10 boxes

# 3\. Scaling and Calculation Logic

# Automated Weight Scaling: The backend must automatically calculate food weights based on the guest count (e.g., if 10 people require 1kg of Biryani, the system must automatically update to 2kg if 20 people are selected)

# Quantity Increments: Guest counts should be adjustable in increments of five (e.g., 10, 15, 20, etc.)

# Staff Allocation: The system must automatically calculate the required number of service staff based on the guest count and the selected pricing tier

# Final Costing: The total price shown to the customer must be a aggregate of the Food Cost, Service Cost, and Transport Cost

# 4\. User Interface and Experience (UI/UX)

# Inclusion Visibility: A pop-up should appear during the "Delivery Only" flow to inform customers of default cutlery provided (plates, bowls, juice, disposables)

# Upselling Flow: Options for "Additional Choices" (e.g., tables, chairs, frill cloths) must be presented at the final stage of the booking to prevent early "price shock" for the user

# Review Phase: Customers must be able to review their finalized menu and customization before proceeding to the final booking.\*\*
